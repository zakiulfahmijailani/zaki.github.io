About Me

I am a lecturer and researcher at Bakrie University, Jakarta, specialising in agentic GeoAI, spatial data science, and the application of large language models to geographic problems. My work bridges academic research and practical system development for public sector challenges in Indonesia.

My doctoral research focuses on building SIGAPP — an Agentic Geospatial Information System for Education Planning — that combines PostGIS spatial operations, vector embeddings, and autonomous AI agents to support evidence-based decision-making in Eastern Indonesia (Nusa Tenggara Timur).

I hold an MSc from Wageningen University & Research (Netherlands) and have experience in full-stack development (Next.js, TypeScript, PostgreSQL/Supabase), machine learning, NLP pipelines, and big data processing with PySpark.

Beyond research, I teach data structures, statistics, and big data processing; develop educational information systems; and mentor students on software architecture and AI integration.

Focus Areas

Research

Agentic GeoAI

Designing autonomous AI agents that reason over geospatial data, execute spatial queries, and synthesise multi-source geographic evidence for planning decisions.

Spatial Data Science

Advanced spatial analytics using PostGIS, network analysis on geographic vector embeddings, and large-scale geospatial processing pipelines for Indonesia's public sector data.

NLP & LLM Applications

Applying transformer models, vector databases, and prompt engineering to extract geographic insights from unstructured text and integrate language understanding into spatial workflows.

Educational Technology

Building AI-assisted information systems (SIPEKA) and curriculum tools for higher education, exploring how intelligent agents can personalise and scale academic support.

Big Data & ML

Distributed processing with PySpark, statistical modelling, and machine learning for large geospatial and educational datasets, with a focus on evaluation methodologies.

Digital Humanities

Investigating the intersection of computational methods, geographic thinking, and humanistic inquiry — with particular focus on Indonesia's regions and communities.
